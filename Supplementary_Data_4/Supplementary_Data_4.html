Supplementary Data 4

Clustering of RefSeq ER annotation categories across different size and GC content metrics

This figure collection explores how extrachromosomal replicons (ERs), categorized according to RefSeq annotations, distribute across two-dimensional scatter plots built using different size and GC content metrics. Each figure includes only ERs (i.e., all replicons that are smaller than the largest replicon in a genome) from taxonomic groups with at least 100 ERs in the dataset.

Each figure is organized in a consistent 2 × 3 grid layout, where:

Columns represent three alternative measures of replicon size:
– absolute size (bp)
– %gen (size relative to total genome size)
– %chr (size relative to chromosome size)

Rows represent two measures of GC content:
– GC content (%)
– ΔGC (%) (difference from the chromosome GC content)

ERs are color-coded by their RefSeq-based annotation type, including secondary chromosomes (defined as all ERs whose name contains the word "chromosome" in the RefSeq replicon name, including chromosome II, chromosome III, secondary/tertiary chromosomes, linear chromosomes in Agrobacterium, etc.), megaplasmids, plasmids, and non-determined elements. This visualization allows evaluation of whether these depositor-assigned categories show clustering patterns under different metric combinations.
